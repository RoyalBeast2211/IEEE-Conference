About Nagpur

Nagpur, located at the geographical center of India, is the winter capital of Maharashtra and one of the fastest-growing cities in Central India. Founded in the 18th century by the Gond rulers and later developed under the Bhonsle dynasty, Nagpur has evolved into a major center for administration, education, and industry. The city is renowned for its well-planned layout, clean environment, and friendly atmosphere, earning it the title of one of India's most livable cities.

Culturally and historically rich, Nagpur reflects a unique blend of traditions. It is home to Deekshabhoomi, a significant monument of social reform and Buddhist heritage, and Sitabuldi Fort, a symbol of the city's Maratha and British past. The city also celebrates numerous festivals such as Ganesh Chaturthi, Diwali, and the Kalidas Festival, showcasing its artistic and cultural diversity.

VNIT is located in the southern part of Nagpur, surrounded by several scenic and popular destinations. Nearby attractions include Ambazari Lake and Futala Lake, both known for their tranquil settings and evening gatherings; Seminary Hills, a lush green viewpoint offering panoramic city views; and Japanese Garden, a peaceful spot for recreation and photography. Other notable landmarks such as the Nagpur Central Museum, Raman Science Centre, and Maharaj Bagh Zoo offer insights into the region's heritage, science, and biodiversity.

With its blend of culture, history, and modern infrastructure, Nagpur provides a welcoming and inspiring environment for academic and professional events.
